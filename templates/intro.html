Press the Shutter

A camera, in one breath.

You squeeze the button.

The Camera Iris snaps to its chosen diameter. The shutter lifts, and for exactly the length of the Capture Duration, light rains onto the sensor. The sensor is a grid of millions of little buckets — each one catches photons. The curtain falls.

The Sensor Sensitivity you set decides how big those buckets are — bigger buckets fill faster in dim light but also catch more noise; smaller ones keep the image clean but need a brighter scene.

All of this in milliseconds. The photo is the record of that brief conversation between the scene and the sensor.

Why this page is dark

Lightroom, Premiere, DaVinci Resolve, the photographer's darkroom — all dark for the same reason: your own iris. A dark UI lets your pupil open wider so your eyes pick up subtle differences in tone. Same reason movie theaters are black. Your eyes are doing photography on this page too.
